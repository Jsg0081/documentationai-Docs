## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  To get started with Snappytb, ensure you have:

  - Beta access (request at [snappytb.com/auth/signup](https://www.snappytb.com/auth/signup))

  - A prepared trial balance file in CSV or Excel format

  - A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Safari recommended)
</Callout>

Snappytb streamlines trial balance management with AI-powered mapping to IRS categories. Follow these steps to import your first dataset and generate mappings.

## Step 1: Sign Up and Access the Dashboard

1. Visit [snappytb.com/auth/signup](https://www.snappytb.com/auth/signup) and create your account.

2. Verify your email and log in.

3. You'll land on the dashboard, featuring:

   - Sidebar for projects and entities

   - Main area for imports and mappings

   - Top bar with export options and settings

<Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
  Save your login credentials securely. Enable two-factor authentication in account settings for added security.
</Callout>

## Step 2: Create a New Project and Import Data

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Project" icon="plus" title-type="p">
    Click **New Project** in the dashboard sidebar.

    Enter a project name like `Q4 2024 Trial Balance` and select your entity type (single or multi-entity).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload File" icon="upload" title-type="p">
    Drag and drop your trial balance file or click **Import Trial Balance**.

    Supported formats:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="CSV" icon="file-text">
        Ensure your CSV has columns: `Account Number`, `Account Name`, `Debit`, `Credit`, `Date`.

        ```csv
        Account Number,Account Name,Debit,Credit,Date
        1001,Cash,50000.00,,2024-12-01
        2001,Accounts Payable,,25000.00,2024-12-01
        ```
      </Tab>

      <Tab title="Excel" icon="file-spreadsheet">
        Use `.xlsx` files with a sheet named `Trial Balance`. First row must be headers matching CSV format above.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Import" icon="check-circle" title-type="p">
    Snappytb normalizes data automatically. Review the preview table for accuracy, then click **Confirm Import**.
  </Step>
</Steps>
